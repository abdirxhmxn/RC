About me

My name is Abdirahman Mohamed, and I recently graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from the University of Southern Maine. Along the way, I’ve built a strong foundation in everything from data structures and algorithms to artificial intelligence and systems programming. But for me, learning has never just been about textbooks, rather it’s been about creating. I’ve developed projects like an advanced calculator in Python that can handle complex expressions, and a Java-based dice game with AI-driven gameplay that balances strategy with fun. I also worked as a QA intern at Lingocard, where I tested cross-platform software, tracked bugs, and learned how much teamwork and precision matter in real-world development.

Outside of tech, I’ve invested deeply in leadership and community. I founded and directed a summer camp for kids ages 6 to 16, building a space where sports, Arabic lessons, and Islamic studies came together to teach teamwork, creativity, and personal growth. That experience reminded me that what really matters — whether in coding, teaching, or leading — is creating environments where people can thrive. I’m passionate about technology not just as a career path, but as a way to solve problems and make a difference. My goal is to continue growing as a developer while also building things that leave a lasting impact on both people and communities.
